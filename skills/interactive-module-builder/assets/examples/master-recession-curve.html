1 · Why recession matters

When the rain stops, a river does not. Streamflow falls along a smooth, repeatable curve called the recession — the signature of water draining slowly out of catchment storage. Its shape tells you how much groundwater a basin holds and how long it can sustain flow through a drought.

Key idea: recession is emptying, not refilling. The slower the recession, the larger and more sluggish the storage feeding the stream.

2 · A draining store

Picture catchment storage as a leaky tank: the more water it holds, the faster it leaks. That single rule — outflow proportional to storage — produces an exponential decline. Drag the recession constant and watch the tank empty.

    'axes.edgecolor':(125/255,211/255,252/255,0.30),'legend.facecolor':'#1a1a2e'})

rng = np.random.default_rng(7)
t = np.arange(60)
k = {{kfit}}                                  # ← substituted live by bindParam
Q0 = 42.0
Q = Q0*np.exp(-k*t) + rng.normal(0, 0.6, t.size)   # noisy observed recession
fit = Q0*np.exp(-k*t)                          # smooth recession model
bfi = fit.sum()/np.clip(Q, 0.1, None).sum()

fig, ax = plt.subplots(figsize=(11, 4))
ax.scatter(t, Q, s=14, color='#00A3FF', label='Observed flow')
ax.plot(t, fit, color='#00DDFF', lw=2, label=f'Recession  k={k:.3f} d⁻¹')
ax.fill_between(t, 0, fit, color='#00DDFF', alpha=0.10)
ax.set_xlabel('Days since rain'); ax.set_ylabel('Q (m³/s)')
ax.set_title(f'Baseflow index ≈ {bfi:.2f}', color='#e2e8f0'); ax.legend()
plt.tight_layout()

6 · Summary

  • Recession is the exponential decay of streamflow as catchment storage drains: Q(t)=Q₀e^(−kt).
  • The recession constant k is an inverse memory: small k → long, drought-resistant baseflow.
  • The baseflow index summarises how much of total flow is delivered slowly through storage.
